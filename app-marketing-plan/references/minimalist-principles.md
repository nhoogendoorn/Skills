## 1. The 8 Principles

### Community First
Does this serve your community? Or is it driven by ego, vanity metrics, or what "successful companies" do? Stay close to your customers. Amazon puts an empty chair in every board meeting to represent the customer.

### Start Manual, Then Automate
Are you over-building? Could this be done manually first? "Processize" before you "productize." Have you done this by hand enough times to know it works?

### Build as Little as Possible
Can you ship this in a weekend? What's the simplest version that makes someone's life better? Are you building for today's customers or hypothetical future ones?

### Sell Before You Scale
Have real people paid real money? Are you trying to market before you've sold? Manual sales = 99% of early growth.

### Spend Time Before Money
Can you do this with time instead of money? Blog posts, social media, personal outreach are free. Only spend money to accelerate what's already working organically.

### Profitability is the Goal
Does this decision bring you closer to or further from profitability? Are you "default alive" or "default dead"? Avoid irreversible decisions (long leases, big hires, VC terms).

### Grow at the Speed of Your Customers
Are your customers asking for this? Your company will grow as quickly as your customers determine. The vast majority of small businesses are never eaten by big fish.

### Build the House You Want to Live In
Does this align with your values? Would you want to work at this company in 5 years? Are you building a business that doesn't own you?

## 2. Decision Framework

For any decision, evaluate:

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| Does this serve my community/customers? | Prevents ego-driven decisions |
| Is this the simplest approach? | Avoids over-engineering |
| Does this improve profitability? | Keeps you alive |
| Is this reversible if it doesn't work? | Limits downside risk |
| Am I spending time or money? | Time first, money only to accelerate |
| Have customers asked for this? | Prevents building for ghosts |
| Does this align with my values? | Ensures long-term sustainability |
| Will I still want this in a year? | Filters short-term temptations |

## 3. Community-First Foundation

### Finding Your Community
1. What communities are you already a part of? (professional groups, hobby communities, online forums, local organizations)
2. Where do you spend your time online? (Reddit, Discord, Slack, Twitter/X, forums)
3. What problems do you hear people complain about repeatedly?
4. Which communities would you be excited to serve for years?

### Evaluating a Community
- **Genuine membership**: You understand the language, values, culture. Contributing, not just lurking.
- **Painful problem**: Would people exchange money for a solution?
- **Reachable**: You know where they gather. You can contact them directly.
- **Right size**: Niche you can dominate, not so broad you'll never stand out.

### Anti-Patterns
- Inventing a community from scratch rather than joining existing one
- Choosing community purely for market size rather than genuine interest
- Skipping participation and jumping straight to "what can I sell"
- Targeting too broad an audience

## 4. Marketing Philosophy

### Marketing is Sales at Scale
Don't confuse marketing with advertising. Marketing is about making fans, not headlines. Start by spending time, not money.

### Prerequisites for Marketing
Before marketing, you should have:
- A community you belong to
- A product people are paying for
- ~100 customers (repeat customers = product-market fit)
- Experience selling one-on-one

### The Three Levels of Content
Progress through these levels, each reaching a wider audience:

**Level 1: Educate** -- Share what you've learned. If you have 100 customers, you've learned 100 things worth sharing. Doesn't need to be polished -- just consistent.

**Level 2: Inspire** -- Go beyond teaching into motivating. Share your journey, struggles, successes. Be vulnerable and authentic. Document progress, not just expertise.

**Level 3: Entertain** -- The hardest but most far-reaching. Entertainment is the king of content on every platform.

### Build in Public
Share what you're working on, what you've improved, what you've learned. Be authentic. Focus on ideas, not self-promotion. Trust the feedback loop.

### Email Over Social Media
- Social media = rented land (algorithms change, accounts get shut down)
- Email = owned land (direct line, no algorithm)
- Each email subscriber is worth far more than a social media follower
- Offer something valuable in exchange for an email

### Spend Money Last
Most growth you see is paid for -- don't be fooled. Only spend on ads after organic traction and known customer profile. Spend on retention before acquisition.

## 5. Common Minimalist Advice

- "Don't launch. Sell to your first 100 customers first."
- "Hire when it hurts."
- "Your failures will fade, while your successes will stick around and compound."
- "Profitability gets you off the grid. Then you grow mindfully with unlimited runway."
- "Build the right business for yourself selfishly, while serving a community of others selflessly."
- "Skip the launch. Focus on selling."
- "There is no such thing as viral success."
