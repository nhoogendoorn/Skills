## 1. Voice vs. Tone

- **Voice** is your consistent personality -- it doesn't change. It's who you are.
- **Tone** shifts by context -- a support reply is warmer than a legal notice, but both sound like you.

---

## 2. Voice Attribute Framework

Define voice with a "We are / We are not" table:

| We are | We are not |
|--------|------------|
| Friendly | Sycophantic |
| Clear | Dumbed down |
| Confident | Arrogant |
| Helpful | Pushy |
| Honest | Blunt |

For each attribute, add an operational definition:
- **Friendly**: We use casual language, address users directly, and acknowledge their frustrations before solving them.
- **Clear**: We use short sentences, common words, and explain technical concepts with analogies.

## 3. Formality Spectrum

Position your brand on this spectrum:

| Level | Example |
|-------|---------|
| **Very casual** | "Yo, your report's ready. Go check it out." |
| **Casual** | "Your report is ready! Take a look when you get a chance." |
| **Conversational** | "Your report is ready. You can view it in your dashboard." |
| **Professional** | "Your report has been generated and is available in your dashboard." |
| **Formal** | "Your analytics report has been successfully generated. Please access it via the dashboard portal." |

Most apps land between casual and professional. Pick the level that matches your audience.

## 4. Tone Shifts by Context

Same voice, different tone across touchpoints:

### App Store Listing
Purpose: Attract and convert. Tone: Confident, benefit-focused, slightly aspirational. Lead with the outcome, not the feature.

### In-App Paywall
Purpose: Upgrade conversion. Tone: Helpful, value-oriented, zero pressure. Show what they gain, make saying "no" easy.

### Social Media
Purpose: Engage and grow audience. Tone: Authentic, educational, personality-forward. Share insights, not pitches.

### Email Newsletter
Purpose: Nurture relationship. Tone: Personal, generous, story-driven. Write like you're emailing one person.

### Support/Error Messages
Purpose: Help and reassure. Tone: Empathetic, clear, solution-oriented. Acknowledge the frustration, then fix it.

### Landing Page
Purpose: Convert visitors. Tone: Clear, specific, proof-heavy. Every sentence should move them toward the CTA.

## 5. Vocabulary Guide Template

### Preferred Words
Words that reinforce your brand personality:
- Use "help" not "empower" (natural vs. corporate)
- Use "simple" not "streamlined" (concrete vs. vague)
- Use "people" not "users" (human vs. clinical)

### Banned Words
Words that contradict your voice:
- Buzzwords: "synergy," "leverage," "disrupt," "revolutionary"
- Hedging: "arguably," "relatively," "somewhat"
- Superlatives without proof: "best," "fastest," "most powerful"

### Jargon Policy
- Use technical terms only when your audience uses them
- Always explain on first use if there's any doubt
- App store copy: zero jargon. In-app for power users: jargon acceptable.

## 6. Example Rewrite Framework

Take a generic sentence and rewrite it in your brand voice:

**Generic**: "Our application utilizes advanced algorithms to optimize your workflow efficiency."

**Casual rewrite**: "We use smart tech to save you time on the boring stuff."

**Conversational rewrite**: "Our app learns your patterns and cuts out the busywork."

**Professional rewrite**: "Built-in intelligence adapts to your workflow and removes repetitive tasks."

Create 3 rewrites for each key message to calibrate your voice.

---

## 7. Consistency Checks

After writing all deliverables, verify:
- [ ] Same personality comes through in every document
- [ ] Formality level is consistent (no sudden shifts from casual to corporate)
- [ ] Vocabulary choices match the preferred/banned word lists
- [ ] Tone shifts are intentional (context-appropriate) not accidental
- [ ] Brand personality adjectives are evident in the writing, not just stated
