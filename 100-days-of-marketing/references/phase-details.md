# Phase Details

Detailed daily breakdowns, evaluation criteria, and frameworks for each phase. The SKILL.md references this file for phase-specific guidance.

## Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1-20)

### Idea Validation (Days 1-5)

Use this framework if the user has <100 customers. Skip to channel setup if already validated.

#### Step 1: Define the Problem
Ask the user to complete these statements:
- "{Specific person} struggles with {specific problem}"
- "Today they solve it by {current workaround}"
- "This costs them {time/money/frustration} because {reason}"

If they can't fill these in with specifics, the idea needs more research.

#### Step 2: Check for Green Flags
At least 3 of these should be true:
- [ ] People are already paying for inferior solutions
- [ ] You've manually solved this for a few people and they valued it
- [ ] The community is actively complaining about this problem
- [ ] You can describe the customer and their pain in one sentence
- [ ] You're scratching your own itch

#### Step 3: Check for Red Flags
If any of these are true, pause and reconsider:
- [ ] Nobody is currently trying to solve this problem (no existing workarounds)
- [ ] You can't name 10 specific people who have this problem
- [ ] The only validation is "my friends think it's a cool idea"
- [ ] You need to educate people that they have this problem
- [ ] You're building for a community you don't belong to

#### Step 4: Pre-Build Questions
1. Can I ship a first version in a weekend? (If no, scope is too big)
2. Will it make someone's life a little better? (If unsure, talk to more people)
3. Would someone pay for this? (If no, find a problem they will pay to solve)
4. Can I get feedback quickly? (If no, find a faster feedback loop)

### Community Identification (Days 3-7)

#### Discovery Questions
Walk through these honestly:
1. What communities am I already part of? (Professional groups, hobby communities, online forums, local orgs, alumni networks, etc.)
2. Where do I spend time online? (Reddit, Discord, Slack, Twitter, forums, YouTube communities)
3. What problems do I hear people complain about repeatedly?
4. Which of these communities would I be excited to serve for years?

#### Evaluation Matrix

Rate each potential community 1-5:

| Criteria | Community A | Community B | Community C |
|----------|------------|------------|------------|
| **Genuine membership**: Am I a real member, not just lurking? | | | |
| **Pain level**: Would people pay to solve this problem? | | | |
| **Reachability**: Do I know where they gather? Can I contact them? | | | |
| **Size**: Niche enough to dominate, large enough to sustain a business? | | | |
| **Total** | | | |

Pick the highest-scoring community. If tied, pick the one where you have the strongest relationships.

#### Anti-Patterns
- Don't invent a community from scratch — join an existing one
- Don't choose purely by market size — genuine interest matters
- Don't skip participation and jump to selling
- Don't target "everyone" — be specific

### Channel Setup (Days 8-14)

#### Checklist
- [ ] Create business social account on primary platform
- [ ] Update personal social bio to mention what you're building
- [ ] Choose and set up email provider (Buttondown, ConvertKit, Mailchimp — pick one)
- [ ] Create lead magnet (checklist, guide, template, or early access waitlist)
- [ ] Set up landing page (even a simple one — Carrd, Framer, or custom)
- [ ] Claim your App Store listing if not yet submitted

#### Platform Selection Guide

| Platform | Best for | Posting cadence | Content type |
|----------|---------|----------------|-------------|
| **Twitter/X** | Dev community, tech audience, build-in-public | Daily | Short insights, threads, screenshots |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional audience, B2B, career-adjacent apps | 3-5x/week | Stories, lessons, product updates |
| **YouTube** | Tutorial-heavy apps, visual products | 1-2x/week | Demos, tutorials, behind-the-scenes |
| **Instagram** | Visual apps, lifestyle-adjacent products | 3-5x/week | Screenshots, stories, reels |
| **Blog** | SEO, evergreen content, detailed tutorials | 2x/week | Long-form how-to, comparisons |

Pick ONE primary platform. Add a blog if you have time. Don't try to be everywhere.

## Phase 2: Content & Community (Days 21-50)

### Content Ramp-Up Schedule

| Week | Blog posts | Social posts | Emails | Focus |
|------|-----------|-------------|--------|-------|
| 4 | 1 | 3-5 | 1 welcome | Start publishing. Quality > quantity. |
| 5 | 2 | 5 | 1 newsletter | Build rhythm. Experiment with formats. |
| 6 | 2 | 5-7 | 1 newsletter | Add sales outreach alongside content. |
| 7 | 2 | 5-7 | 1 newsletter | Double down on top-performing content. |

### Three Levels of Content — Example Ideas

#### Educate (share what you've learned)
1. "How I {solved specific problem} in {app/domain}"
2. "{X} tips for {target audience} to {achieve outcome}"
3. "The complete guide to {topic your app addresses}"
4. "Common mistakes {audience} makes with {topic}"
5. "{Tool/framework} comparison for {use case}"

#### Inspire (share your journey)
1. "Why I quit {thing} to build {app}"
2. "Month {X} revenue report: ${amount} MRR"
3. "The feedback that changed everything about {app}"
4. "What I learned from my first {X} customers"
5. "The hardest decision I made building {app}"

#### Entertain (make it memorable)
1. "{Relatable struggle} as an indie developer" (meme format)
2. "Things only {your audience} will understand"
3. "My setup vs. my users' expectations" (humor)
4. "The {app category} alignment chart"
5. "POV: You just shipped a feature nobody asked for"

### Community Engagement Playbook

**The Contribute-to-Sell Ratio**: For every 1 promotional post, share 9 non-promotional contributions. Comment on others' work, answer questions, share resources.

**How to share without spamming**:
- Answer questions where your app is a genuine answer
- Share the insight, not the product: "I learned X while building for Y"
- Ask for feedback, not downloads: "What do you think of this approach?"
- Be helpful first: solve their problem even if your app isn't the answer

### Marketing Funnel Mapping

| Funnel Stage | Content Type | Goal |
|-------------|-------------|------|
| **Engage** | Social posts, blog titles, tweets | Get noticed |
| **Follow** | Consistent content, personality, value | Earn a follow/subscribe |
| **Research** | Landing page, App Store listing, comparisons | Answer "is this for me?" |
| **Consider** | Testimonials, case studies, free trial | Remove doubts |
| **Buy** | Clear CTA, pricing transparency, risk reversal | Convert |

## Phase 3: Launch & Sales (Days 51-80)

### Concentric Circles Sales Playbook

#### Circle 1: Friends & Family (Days 35-41)
- Make a list of 10 people who trust you
- Pitch them on being your first customers, not investors
- Ask for honest feedback, not social media posts
- If they won't buy, ask why — that feedback is gold

#### Circle 2: Community (Days 42-55)
- List everyone who has written about or used similar tools
- Contact each personally — walk them through your app
- Offer a free trial, discount, or personal onboarding
- Ask for candid feedback after they try it

#### Circle 3: Strangers (Days 56-80)
- Find people who could benefit by searching forums, social media, blog comments
- Send personalized cold emails (see content-templates.md)
- Each rejection refines your pitch — track what works
- Follow up once after 5-7 days, then stop

### Pricing Guidance

- **The zero-price effect**: There's a massive gap between free and $1. Charge something.
- **Cost-based pricing**: Your costs + 20-50% margin. Simple but leaves money on the table.
- **Value-based pricing**: What is it worth to the customer? A time-tracking app that saves 5 hours/week is worth more than $5/month.
- **Start low, raise over time**: Price is iterative. Current customers keep their rate; new customers pay more.
- **Indie app pricing reality**: Most successful indie apps charge $5-15/month or $30-80 one-time. Don't underprice.

### Launch Checklist (After ~100 Customers)

Launch is a celebration, not a customer acquisition strategy.

- [ ] Thank your existing customers publicly
- [ ] Write a launch blog post telling the story
- [ ] Post on Product Hunt, Hacker News, or relevant community (pick one)
- [ ] Send a launch email to your list
- [ ] Share on all social channels
- [ ] Update App Store promotional text
- [ ] Monitor reviews and respond within 24 hours

## Phase 4: Scale & Optimize (Days 81-100)

### Minimalist Review Framework

Evaluate every channel, tactic, and expense against these 8 principles:

| Principle | Question | Your Answer |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **Community first** | Does this serve my community, or just my ego? | |
| **Start manual, then automate** | Am I over-building? Could I do this by hand first? | |
| **Build as little as possible** | Is this the simplest version that works? | |
| **Sell before you scale** | Have real people paid for this? | |
| **Spend time before money** | Can I do this with time instead of money? | |
| **Profitability is the goal** | Does this bring me closer to profitability? | |
| **Grow at your customers' speed** | Are my customers asking for this? | |
| **Build what you want to live in** | Will I want this in a year? Does it align with my values? | |

### Decision Framework

For any optimization decision (new channel, paid ads, feature, partnership), answer:

1. Does this serve my community/customers?
2. Is this the simplest approach?
3. Does this improve profitability?
4. Is this reversible if it doesn't work?
5. Am I spending time or money?
6. Have customers asked for this?
7. Does this align with my values?
8. Will I still want this in a year?

If you answer "no" to 3+ questions, don't do it. Simplify instead.

### When to Spend Money on Ads

Only consider paid advertising when ALL of these are true:
- [ ] You have organic traction (people find and buy without ads)
- [ ] You know your customer profile precisely
- [ ] You have a positive unit economics (LTV > CAC)
- [ ] You can spend less than you make per customer

**Where to start**:
- Apple Search Ads: High intent, targets people searching the App Store
- Lookalike audiences: Facebook/Instagram can find people similar to your existing customers
- Retargeting: Show ads to people who visited your site but didn't convert

**Guardrails**:
- Set a daily budget cap — never spend open-ended
- Measure ROI weekly — kill underperforming campaigns fast
- Spend money on existing customers (loyalty, referrals) before spending on acquisition

### Planning the Next 100 Days

At day 100, create a brief document answering:

1. **Top 3 channels by ROI** (where do customers actually come from?)
2. **Top 3 content pieces by engagement** (what resonated most?)
3. **Biggest surprise** (what worked or failed unexpectedly?)
4. **Biggest waste of time** (what should you stop doing?)
5. **Next milestone** (what's the goal for the next 100 days?)
6. **One thing to double down on** (the single highest-leverage activity)
7. **One thing to try** (a new experiment for the next cycle)
