# Copy Guidelines

Writing rules, headline formulas, and CTA patterns for all marketing copy in the 100-day plan. Every piece of copy — blog post, tweet, email, landing page — should follow these guidelines, filtered through the user's tone-of-voice.md.

---

## 6 Core Style Rules

### 1. Simple over complex
Use everyday words. If a simpler word exists, use it.

| Instead of | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| Utilize | Use |
| Facilitate | Help |
| Leverage | Use |
| Implement | Set up |
| Commence | Start |

### 2. Specific over vague
Avoid words that sound good but mean nothing.

| Vague (avoid) | Specific (use) |
|---------------|----------------|
| Streamline your workflow | Cut your weekly reporting from 4 hours to 15 minutes |
| Innovative solution | The only Mac app that does {X} without {Y} |
| Optimize your productivity | Finish your task list before lunch |
| Best-in-class | Rated 4.8 stars by 2,000 users |

### 3. Active over passive
The subject does the action.

| Passive (avoid) | Active (use) |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Reports are generated automatically | The app generates your reports automatically |
| Your data is protected | We encrypt your data end-to-end |

### 4. Confident over qualified
Remove hedging words.

| Hedging (avoid) | Confident (use) |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| We can almost guarantee | We guarantee |
| It's very easy to use | It's easy to use |
| You'll really love it | You'll love it |
| It basically does X | It does X |

### 5. Show over tell
Describe the outcome instead of using empty adverbs.

| Tell (avoid) | Show (use) |
|-------------|-----------|
| Incredibly fast | Syncs in under 2 seconds |
| Super easy | Set up in 3 taps |
| Instantly available | Ready before you finish your coffee |

### 6. Honest over sensational
Never fabricate numbers, testimonials, or claims. If you don't have proof, don't claim it. Understated truth builds more trust than exaggerated hype.

## Headline Formulas

Use these as starting points. Adapt to match the user's tone-of-voice.md.

### Benefit-focused
- **{Achieve outcome} without {pain point}**
  → "Track every expense without opening a spreadsheet"
- **{Achieve outcome} in {timeframe}**
  → "Organize your photos in under 5 minutes"
- **{Key benefit of your product}**
  → "Sound clear in online meetings"

### Audience-focused
- **{Product type} for {specific audience}**
  → "A writing app for novelists who hate distractions"
- **{Product} for {audience} to {outcome}**
  → "An online whiteboard for teams to brainstorm together"

### Pain-focused
- **Never {unpleasant thing} again**
  → "Never lose a receipt again"
- **{Question highlighting pain point}**
  → "Tired of managing passwords in a spreadsheet?"
- **Stop {pain}. Start {pleasure}.**
  → "Stop guessing your finances. Start knowing."

### Differentiator-focused
- **The {opposite of usual} way to {outcome}**
  → "The effortless way to track your habits"
- **Turn {input} into {output}**
  → "Turn your meeting notes into action items"
- **You don't have to {hard thing} to {outcome}**
  → "You don't have to be a designer to make beautiful presentations"

### Social proof
- **{Number} {people} use {product} to {outcome}**
  → "5,000 freelancers use InvoiceBot to get paid faster"
- **The {category} that {differentiator}**
  → "The to-do app that actually gets used"

## CTA Formulas

### The Pattern
**[Action Verb] + [What They Get] + [Qualifier if needed]**

### Strong CTAs
| CTA | Why it works |
|-----|-------------|
| Start my free trial | First person, low risk, specific |
| Download for Mac | Platform-specific, clear action |
| Get the complete checklist | Specific deliverable |
| Try it free — no account needed | Removes friction |
| See how it works | Low commitment, curiosity |

### Weak CTAs (avoid)
| CTA | Why it fails |
|-----|-------------|
| Submit | Robotic, no value |
| Sign Up | What am I signing up for? |
| Learn More | Vague, passive |
| Click Here | Tells them nothing |
| Get Started | Get started doing what? |

### CTA Placement
- **Above the fold**: Primary CTA after the headline and subheadline
- **After benefits section**: Repeat CTA once the value is clear
- **End of page**: Final CTA with risk reversal (guarantee, free trial, no credit card)
- **In content**: Contextual CTAs within blog posts and emails — natural, not forced

## Landing Page Structure

The recommended section order for your app's landing page. Not every section is required — pick the ones that fit your stage.

### Minimum Viable Landing Page (Phase 1)
1. **Hero**: Headline + subheadline + CTA + screenshot
2. **Problem**: 2-3 pain points your audience recognizes
3. **Solution**: How your app solves them (3 key benefits)
4. **How it works**: 3 simple steps
5. **CTA**: Repeat with risk reversal

### Full Landing Page (Phase 3+)
1. **Hero**: Headline, subheadline, primary CTA, product screenshot
2. **Social proof bar**: Logos, star rating, user count, or press mentions
3. **Problem section**: Articulate the pain — "You know the feeling..."
4. **How it works**: 3-4 numbered steps
5. **Key benefits**: 2-3 benefits with icons, not 10 features
6. **Testimonial**: Specific quote with name, role, and result
7. **Use cases or personas**: "Built for {type of person}"
8. **Comparison**: How you differ from alternatives (be honest)
9. **Case study snippet**: One customer story with a metric
10. **FAQ**: Address 5-7 common objections
11. **Final CTA**: Recap value + CTA + guarantee/risk reversal

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing any copy, scan for:

- [ ] **Jargon**: Would an outsider understand every word?
- [ ] **Overloaded sentences**: Any sentence doing more than one job? Split it.
- [ ] **Passive voice**: Rewrite any "is/was/were [verb]ed" constructions
- [ ] **Exclamation points**: Remove them. Your copy should be exciting without punctuation tricks.
- [ ] **Buzzwords**: Replace any instance of "innovative," "cutting-edge," "revolutionary," "seamless," "robust" with something specific
- [ ] **Tone match**: Does this sound like the person described in tone-of-voice.md?
