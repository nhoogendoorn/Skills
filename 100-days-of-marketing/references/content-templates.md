# Content Templates

Ready-to-adapt templates for every content type in the 100-day plan. Customize all templates using the user's tone-of-voice.md and app details from marketing.md.

---

## Blog Post Templates

### Template 1: Educational How-To
**Use in**: Phase 2-3, educate content level

```
Title: How to {solve specific problem} with {approach or tool}

Hook (2-3 sentences): State the problem your audience faces. Make them nod.

The Problem:
- Describe the pain point in their language
- Quantify the cost (time, money, frustration)

The Solution:
- Step-by-step walkthrough (3-5 steps)
- Include screenshots or examples
- Mention your app naturally where relevant — don't force it

Key Takeaway:
- One-sentence summary of what they learned

CTA: {action that leads to your app or email list}
```

**Word count**: 500-800 words. Quality over length.

### Template 2: Build-in-Public Update
**Use in**: Phase 2-4, inspire content level

```
Title: {App Name} Week {X}: {one honest insight}

What happened this week:
- Key metric or milestone (downloads, revenue, feedback)
- One thing that worked
- One thing that didn't

What I learned:
- The surprising takeaway
- How it changes my approach

What's next:
- One specific thing you'll try next week

CTA: Follow along / subscribe for updates
```

**Word count**: 300-500 words. Authenticity matters more than polish.

### Template 3: Comparison / Alternative Post
**Use in**: Phase 2-3, educate content level. Great for SEO.

```
Title: {Your App} vs {Competitor}: {honest differentiator}

Context: Why someone would compare these two apps

{Competitor} is great for:
- Genuine strengths (be honest — builds trust)

{Your App} is better for:
- Specific use cases where you win
- Back each claim with a concrete example

The bottom line:
- One-sentence recommendation based on the reader's situation

CTA: Try {app} free / See for yourself
```

**Word count**: 600-1000 words. Be genuinely helpful, not salesy.

## LinkedIn Templates

### Template 1: Story Hook
```
I almost {dramatic statement about your app journey}.

Here's what happened:
{3-5 lines telling the story}

The lesson:
{One takeaway relevant to your audience}

{Question to drive engagement}
```

### Template 2: Lesson Learned
```
{X} months building {app name}. Here's what I wish I knew on day 1:

1. {Lesson} — {one-line explanation}
2. {Lesson} — {one-line explanation}
3. {Lesson} — {one-line explanation}

The biggest surprise? {Unexpected insight}

What's your experience with {topic}?
```

### Template 3: Product Update
```
New in {app name}: {feature in benefit language}

The problem: {what users struggled with}
The fix: {what you built, in simple terms}
The result: {outcome for users}

Try it: {link}
```

**LinkedIn tips**: First line is everything — it determines if people click "see more." No hashtag spam. 1-2 relevant hashtags max.

## Email Templates

### Welcome Sequence (3 emails)

**Email 1: Immediate welcome**
```
Subject: Welcome — here's your {lead magnet}

Hi {name},

Thanks for signing up. Here's {the thing you promised}: {link}

Quick intro: I'm {name}, and I built {app} because {one-sentence origin story}.

Over the next few emails, I'll share {what they'll get — tips, behind-the-scenes, etc.}

Talk soon,
{name}
```

**Email 2: Day 3 — Value email**
```
Subject: {Specific tip related to their problem}

Hi {name},

{Teach them something useful in 3-5 paragraphs}

{Mention your app only if it naturally fits}

{name}

P.S. Hit reply if you have questions — I read every email.
```

**Email 3: Day 7 — Soft sell**
```
Subject: The {problem} nobody talks about

Hi {name},

{Describe a common struggle your audience faces}

{How your app addresses it — specific, not vague}

{CTA: try it free / see how it works}

{name}
```

```
Subject: {One compelling insight or number}

💡 {One tip or insight}
```

## Cold Outreach Templates

### Personalized Email
```
Subject: {Something specific about their work}

Hi {name},

I noticed you {specific thing you observed — their blog post, tweet, project, complaint}.

I built {app name} which {one-sentence description of how it helps with their specific situation}.

Would love to show you — or just share some {relevant resource} we put together. No pressure either way.

{Your name}
{One-line credibility — "I've been building iOS apps for X years" or similar}
```

**Rules**:
- Each email must reference something specific about the recipient
- Never copy-paste the same email twice
- Each rejection teaches you something — refine the next email
- Follow up once after 5-7 days, then stop

### Follow-Up Email
```
Subject: Re: {original subject}

Hi {name},

Just bumping this — I know inboxes get busy.

{One new thing since your last email — a feature, a customer win, a relevant insight}

Happy to chat or just send over {resource}. Either way, no worries.

{name}
```

## Build-in-Public Post Ideas

Use these prompts when you're stuck on what to share:

1. **Revenue milestone**: "Hit ${X} MRR this month. Here's what's working..."
2. **Failed experiment**: "Tried {thing}. It completely flopped. Here's why..."
3. **Customer feedback**: "A user said {quote}. It changed how I think about {feature}..."
4. **Decision made**: "Had to choose between {A} and {B}. Went with {A} because..."
5. **Tool discovered**: "Found {tool} and it cut my {task} time in half..."
6. **Metric shared**: "Last 30 days: {X} downloads, {Y} trials, {Z} conversions. Breakdown..."
7. **Behind the scenes**: "Here's what my morning looks like as a solo dev..."
8. **Mistake admitted**: "I wasted {time} on {thing}. Should have just {simpler approach}..."
9. **Feature rationale**: "Why I built {feature} instead of {other feature}..."
10. **Gratitude post**: "A customer just {positive thing}. This is why I build software."
